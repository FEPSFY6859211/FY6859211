Abstract

         The use of unmanned autonomous systems has been transforming so many industries in today’s world, 
         allowing for better operational efficiency, their capabilities and precision. They fulfil complex 
         tasks without the use of human involvement, and their uses range in industries such as medicine and 
         agriculture, to military use and aircraft. For the medical sector, the AtraxM drone in Poland helps 
         with emergency situations, being able to help locate people in large disasters and transport medical 
         equipment to harsh terrains where it would initially be inaccessible. In agriculture, these systems 
         are installed to help farmers with harvesting, pest control efficiency and sowing. Meanwhile, for militaries, 
         it increases operational efficiency in factories to produce new-generation UAVs to help prevent future crises 
         from happening, and for the aircraft industry, AI-powered platforms are used to monitor the status of aircraft, 
         allowing for optimal aircraft safety.

Introduction

Technology advancements in today’s modern world has reshaped the way we all live specifically unmanned autonomous systems. These are man-made systems, that can carry out complex tasks with extreme efficiency and precision without human intervention (Zhang, T. et al. (2017)). Their applications range from agricultural tasks, where drones are used for monitoring crops; medicines, where drones are used to support in rescue operations (Konert, Smereka and Szarpak, 2019)), aircraft (Aviation Week Network, (2023/24)), and finally to militaries across the world.
